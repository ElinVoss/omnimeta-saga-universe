COMMIT_SHA → 3a9c1d9f4e5b6a7c8d90123456789abcdeffedcb

Why pin to a SHA?

- Stability: The Space always loads the exact version used to generate prior outputs.
- Reproducibility: If prose or bibles need to be re-run, the same canon is fetched.

5) When canon updates, make a new commit and update SourceMap_Git.md to the new SHAs; the Space then consumes the updated canon next session.

## 2. THE WORLD: TE'OGA

### A. Planetary Wound: The Bore

**What everyone knows:**

- Six centuries ago, during the God War (called the Severing in historical records), a catastrophic event carved a cylindrical wound—the **Bore**—through one hemisphere of Te'Oga.
- The Bore's exact nature is mysterious and hotly debated: weapon, escape route, prison break, failed seal?
- No one investigates the Bore's origin. Those who ask too many questions disappear.

**Physical consequences:**

- Rivers permanently rerouted.
- Gravity shifts along hemisphere boundaries.
- Pre-Bore technology doesn't function the same way.
- Reality itself feels fragmented—veins (stress channels) permeate all materials and living things.

**Narrative function:**

- The Bore explains the hemispheric split (Underface vs. Outer Crown).
- It justifies the aesthetic dyad: glasspunk and forgepunk coexist because two different survival strategies emerged.
- It anchors the world's scarcity and danger.

**What is NOT known (until N25+):**

- The Bore's true origin.
- Connection to the heat grid.
- Role in pre-Bore cosmology.

### B. Hemispheric Division

#### The Underface (Forgepunk Domain)

**Location:** Beneath primary crust level; vast caverns, exposed bedrock, industrial heat.

**Aesthetic:** Iron, basalt, pressure, muscle, soot.

**Cities:** Grindheim (primary N1–N5 setting), Forgeport, Ash-Hallow.

**Characteristics:**

- Roaring forges at city centers; massive heat sources.

- Pressure engineering: structures designed to withstand/redirect force.

**Society:**

- Guild-based: riggers, engineers, haulers, smiths.
- Quota enforcement: civic bosses extract labor through posted demands (see Ridged Postings below).
- Sanitist control: religious-civic hybrid in power; purity doctrine enforced.
- Found-family networks substitute for blood kinship.
- Survival mentality: no room for abstractions; competency = worth.

***

#### The Outer Crown (Glasspunk Domain)

**Location:** Upper crust level; atmospheric exposure.

**Aesthetic:** Crystal, glass, light, wind, refraction, elegance.

**Cities:** Shatterholm, Prism, Velocity-Heights.

**Characteristics:**

- Vast glass domes: atmospheric shields over settlements.
- Mirrored architecture: structures designed to catch/redirect light.
- Aerial transit: wind-current technologies enable flight.

**Society:**

- Scholar-merchant culture: pilots, architects, traders.

- Outer Crown ideology emphasizes knowledge and aesthetics.
- Inter-city trade networks.

Between Underface and Outer Crown runs **the Curtain**—a violent atmospheric phenomenon:

- Perpetual storm conditions.
- Electromagnetic disturbances.
- Extreme pressure differences.

**Narrative function:**

- Tiffani's origin (Outer Crown-trained healer) explains her different craft style and mysterious training.
- Crossing the Curtain is dangerous but possible; refugees flee through it under crisis.
- The Curtain isolates the two aesthetics, preventing cultural homogenization.

**What everyone knows:**

**What no one investigates:**

- Source of the heat.
- Why pipes don't cool over distance.
- How pre-Bore cities are connected.

**Writer-facing rule:**

**Why this matters (OmniMeta only):**

- Locking investigation creates tension and mystery **without foreshadowing**.

### D. Time Measurement: The Bells

**How time works in Te'Oga:**

- No clocks or mechanical timepieces (pre-industrial society).
- Time marked by **bells** rung from civic centers.

**Bell schedule:**

- **First bell**: Dawn; start of work day.
- **Third bell**: Mid-morning.
- **Fifth bell**: Noon.
- **Seventh bell**: Late afternoon.
- **Ninth bell**: Dusk; end of work day.
- **Eleventh bell**: Deep night (rare for citizens to be awake).

**Civic posting synchronization:**

- Official orders posted when bells ring.
- Everyone hears the bell—no excuse for ignorance.
- Quotas tracked by bell cycles: weekly bells, monthly bells, quarterly bells.

**Narrative function:**

- Bells create urgency without electricity.
- They're tactile, public, shared—characters can't ignore time when the whole city hears it.

### E. Civic Infrastructure: Ridged Postings

**How law works:**

- Instead of written documents, Te'Oga uses **tactile postings**—ridged boards with embossed symbols and indented marks.

**Icon system:**

- **Bell mark** (circular imprint): indicates time/deadline.
- **Quill square** (rectangular indentation): indicates recording/documentation.
- **Ring glyph** (spiral pattern): indicates authority/enforcement.
- **Stamp bite** (deep impression): indicates severity of consequence.

**Example posting:**
> Quota due, fifth bell, first quarter. Grain to civic stores. Shortfall penalty: three days detainment.
> - Bell mark tells **when**.
> - Quill square indicates **it's official record**.
> - Ring glyph shows **Sanitist authority**.
> - Stamp bite suggests **severe enforcement**.

**Accessibility:**

- Citizens read postings by touch as much as sight.
- Blind citizens can navigate law.
- Illiteracy is not an excuse.

## 3. MAGIC SYSTEM: SOULPULSE (INNOCENT FRAME)

### A. Core Philosophy: Craft, Not Song

**THE LAW:**
Magic in The Confluence Chronicles is **tactile engineering**, never musical, never harmonic, never song-based.

**Forbidden language:**

- Ban: "sing," "harmonics," "melody," "symphony," "orchestrated" in magic context.
- Ambient noise (bells ringing, gear grinding, heartbeat in prose) = all permitted.
- **Why?** Song implies external orchestration; craft implies agency and material consequence.

### B. What Is Soulpulse?

**Soulpulse** is the manipulation of **veins**—invisible stress channels in materials and bodies.

- Every object, structure, and living thing contains veins.
- A vein can be **stable, stressed, or rupturing**.
- Craftspeople manipulate veins through deliberate physical action and energy injection.

**Access:**

- Not everyone can wield Soulpulse.
- Eligibility tied to **conviction**—a psychological state of certainty, grounding, and willingness to pay cost.
- Training required: years of practice to sense veins and control energy flow.

**Emotion as fuel:**

- Soulpulse responds to emotional states: grief, love, rage, desperation amplify power.
- Cost increases with emotional intensity—grief-fueled Soulpulse risks Hollowing.

***

### C. The Four Principles (Innocent Frame)

In innocent canon, the Four Principles are **philosophical ideals**—not gods, not cosmic entities, but **ways of thinking about craft and the world**.

#### 1. Form (Corlexi Tradition)

**Philosophy:** Geometric perfection; structures locked into precise patterns.

**Craft application:**

- Form-locking: rigidly stabilizes materials via geometric calculation.
- All-or-nothing approach: perfection or failure, no middle ground.
- Favors precision over flexibility.

**Practitioners:** Tiffani (expert), Sanitist engineers, military architects.

**Cost:** Chronic precision fatigue, frozen hands, inability to deviate from planned action.

***

#### 2. Wholeness (Salee Tradition)

**Philosophy:** Healing integration; mending broken parts into functional wholes.

**Craft application:**

- Wholeness-bracing: stabilizes failing systems by mending veins together.
- Holistic, integrating approach; works to preserve life.
- Compassionate—favors flexibility and adaptation.

**Practitioners:** Jhace (primary), healers, found-family crew.

**Cost:** Tremor, thirst, emotional weight (feels others' pain), personal bleeding when saving others.

***

#### 3. Purity (Mobel Tradition)

**Philosophy:** Cleansing elimination; erase imperfection through absolute action.

**Craft application:**

- Purity-scour: can erase or accelerate decay of structures/bodies.
- Destructive, absolute—no partial application.
- Fanatical approach: complete eradication.

**Practitioners:** Sanitist enforcers, fire-doctrine zealots.

**Cost:** Addiction (users crave the absolute feeling), personality degradation, loss of empathy.

***

#### 4. Entropy (Eries Tradition)

**Philosophy:** Honest decay; accepting and accelerating natural degradation.

**Craft application:**

- Entropy-release: accepts and accelerates collapse; doesn't fight failure.
- Pragmatic approach: work with failure, not against it.
- Release-focused.

**Practitioners:** Philosophers, heat-grid rebels, people seeking peace.

**Cost:** Depression, loss of ambition, difficulty taking action.

***

### D. Dual-Principle Capacity: Jhace's Unique Trait

**Normal practitioners align with ONE principle** (Form, Wholeness, Purity, OR Entropy).

**Why?** Wielding contradictory principles causes resonance collision—veins rupture, channels shred, user dies.

**Jhace's paradox:**

- He wields **Wholeness AND a secondary principle** (later revealed as Decay/Entropy hybrid) **simultaneously**.
- **Rarity:** 1 in 300 people show any capacity; dual-principle users **shouldn't exist**.
- **Why this matters:**
    - Tiffani recognizes this in N1 Ch.2—immediately invested.
    - Foundation for his eventual Fathombreak power (wielding all four simultaneously).
    - **Cost:** Every dual-wielding moment risks his life—channels scream, veins tear.

**Example (N1 Ch.1):**
Jhace holds a failing scaffold with Wholeness while simultaneously using Decay to eat away the rupture point. Should kill him—doesn't. Tiffani watches in shock.

### E. Costs: What Soulpulse Demands

**Primary costs (visible, permanent):**

- **Hand tremor:** After first significant use; worsens with repeated use; semi-permanent.
- **Thirst:** During and after use; becomes chronic with heavy use; semi-permanent.
- **Ear-ring (tinnitus):** After sustained use; can become permanent.
- **Rib pain:** With velding (Outer Crown techniques); Tiffani's chronic pain.
- **Back strain:** After prolonged pressure-balancing; Jhace develops chronic tension by N3.

**Secondary costs (internal, less visible):**

- **Sleep debt:** Night after heavy use; cumulative; compounds next day; temporary but dangerous.
- **Nosebleeds:** During extreme pressure-balancing; indicator of internal resonance damage; temporary but warning sign.
- **Numbness/sensory fade:** After repeated use in same location; **can be permanent** if overuse continues.

**Tertiary costs (extreme, life-threatening):**

- **Hollowing (introduced N4):** Complete resonance burnout; channels shred from overextension; symptoms include nosebleeds, hallucinations, catatonia, potential death; **permanent capacity loss**; recovery uncertain.

**Core truth:** No act of Soulpulse is free. Every use exacts a permanent or semi-permanent physical toll.

# Temporal Lexicon of the Forge-Glass Cultures

## **The Crafted Calendar System**

### **Tide** (Day)

A single rotation of the world, named for the flowing rhythm of molten glass and the inexorable passage of heat through the forge. “Three tides hence” means three days from now.

- *Dawn:* “First Tide” or “Kindling”
- *Dusk:* “Cooling Hour” or “The Quench”


### **Anneal** (Week - 5 days)

The five-day cycle required for proper annealing of significant glassworks. Each day has a craft-name:

1. **Heattide** (preparation, gathering materials)
2. **Pourtide** (beginning work, molten state)
3. **Shapetide** (formation, the peak of creation)
4. **Striketide** (refinement, hammer and tool)
5. **Cooltide** (settling, hardening, rest)

### **Crucible** (Month - 7 anneals / 35 tides)

Seven anneals, representing the seven stages of a master crucible’s use before it must be relined or replaced. The seven anneals within a Crucible are often named:

1. First Heat
2. Rising Glow
3. Full Blaze
4. Steady Burn
5. Ember Anneal
6. Fading Coals
7. Final Temper

### **Casting** (Year - 9 crucibles / 315 tides)

Nine Crucibles make one complete Casting—the time required to cast and complete a grand work incorporating both forged metal framework and blown glass panels. The nine Crucibles are grouped into three seasons:

**First Forge** (Early Season - 3 Crucibles)

- Crucible of Iron Roots
- Crucible of Rising Frames
- Crucible of Skeletal Form

**The Great Blow** (Middle Season - 3 Crucibles)

- Crucible of Molten Sand
- Crucible of Crystal Breath
- Crucible of Prismatic Fire

**Fusion Season** (Late Season - 3 Crucibles)

- Crucible of Marriage (metal meets glass)
- Crucible of Setting
- Crucible of Final Temper


### **Smelting** (Decade - 10 castings / 3,150 tides)

The apprenticeship period, representing ten Castings of learning before a craftsperson achieves journeyman status. “Still in my first smelting” = still young/learning.

### **Masterwork** (Century - 100 castings)

The span of a legendary piece’s creation and reputation. Great monuments are measured in Masterworks. “Built three Masterworks past” = 300 castings ago.

### **Epoch of Fusion** or **The Great Kiln** (Millennium - 1000 castings)

Major eras when revolutionary techniques emerged, typically marked by catastrophic forge-fires or crystalline discoveries that changed civilization.

### **Eon - The Eternal Crucible**

Geological/mythological time beyond measure. The age of the world itself, imagined as an inexhaustible crucible that holds the molten core of reality.

## **Common Phrases:**

- **“Within the tide”** = today
- **“This very tide”** = right now, today
- **“Before the anneal cools”** = within the anneal (5 tides)
- **“A crucible of mourning”** = a crucible of grief (35 tides)
- **“Mid-crucible”** = halfway through the crucible (month)
- **“Three crucibles hence”** = three crucibles from now
- **“Many castings ago”** = many castings (years) ago
- **“A smelting of service”** = a smelting (decade) of work
- **“Not yet through my first anneal”** = less than an anneal old (often said of newborns)
- **“Between the Fifth and Sixth”** = between the 5th and 6th anneal of the crucible
- **“Forged before my smelting”** = before I was born
- **“On Striketide”** = on the fourth tide of the anneal
- **“Meet me at Pourtide”** = meet me on the second tide
- **“In the Third Masterwork of the Glass Rebellion”** = in the 3rd Masterwork of that era
